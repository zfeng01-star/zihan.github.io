2 - A History of HTML

In this page, we take a look at the history of HTML, figure out how it was created, who created it, and how it has developed.

1989: The Invention of the Web and HTML at CERN

Tim Berners-Lee invented the World Wide Web at CERN to facilitate global information sharing among researchers. He created HTML (HyperText Mark-up Language) as its simple publishing language and HTTP as the transfer protocol, implementing his first prototype on a NeXT workstation in 1990.

1990: Building HTML on the SGML Standard

Instead of creating a completely new language, Tim Berners-Lee based HTML's syntax on SGML (Standard Generalized Mark-up Language), an internationally agreed standard for defining document structure. His key innovation was adding the hypertext anchor element (<A> with the HREF attribute) to create links.

September 1991: The Start of Open Collaboration via WWW-talk

The development of HTML became an open, collaborative process when the WWW-talk mailing list was launched. This electronic forum allowed Tim Berners-Lee, early enthusiasts like Dave Raggett, and other researchers to openly debate ideas and the future direction of HTML across the Internet.

December 1992: The Introduction of the <IMG> Tag by Mosaic

A pivotal moment occurred when Marc Andreessen of the NCSA Mosaic browser team introduced the <IMG> tag to embed images. While its design was controversial, its implementation in the Mosaic browser made it a de-facto standard.

April 1993: Release of the NCSA Mosaic Browser

The release of the Mosaic browser for UNIX workstations was a major catalyst for the Web's popularity. It extended HTML with highly attractive features like embedded images, nested lists, and fill-out forms.
